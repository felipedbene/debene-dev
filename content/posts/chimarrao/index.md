description: "A poem about mate, machines, and the things that stay hot at 2 AM."
---

there is something about
the first sip of mate
at 2 AM in a Chicago basement
while a POWER8 compiles
what Microsoft refused to ship

it tastes like her —
bitter and green
and alive in a way
that polite things never are.

you drink it too hot
because you've always been
the kind of idiot
who likes to burn.

she told me once
that my mouth tasted like yerba
and I said baby
that's not the yerba.

the machine hums
160 threads
doing what I told it to
which is more
than I can say
about anything else
in my life.

I pour another round.
the water is never
the right temperature.
the woman is never
coming back.

but the build is still running
and the mate is still hot
and that's enough
for a Friday night
in America.

not Americana, SP.
the other one.
the one that doesn't know
what chimarrão tastes like.
